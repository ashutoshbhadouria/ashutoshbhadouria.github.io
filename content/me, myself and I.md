# research experience

Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN  
Graduate Research Assistant, Aug. 2022 – Present  
Advisor: Profs. Brian M. Tackett and Jeffrey Greeley

- Developed methodologies to characterize room-temperature electrochemical activation of alkanes on metal catalysts using operando mass spectrometry.
- Designed and executed electrochemical X-ray absorption spectroscopy (XAS) experiments at the CNPEM synchrotron (Brazil) to investigate hydride phase formation on Cu(111) electrodes.
- Collaborated with Prof. Jeffrey E. Dick (Purdue University, Department of Chemistry) to perform in-situ analysis of charge dynamics in aqueous Zn-ion batteries.
  

University of California, Berkeley, Berkeley, CA  
Undergraduate Research Assistant, Jan. 2020 – May 2021  
Advisor: Prof. Alexander Katz

- Utilized UV-Vis spectroscopy to determine the rate of photodegradation of organic sunscreen active ingredients in the presence of TiO₂ and a cerium-based photoprotective catalyst.
- Determined orders of magnitude of stability offered by a novel photocatalyst via a study of first-order reaction kinetics.
- Constructed MATLAB data analytical models to study efficacy of active ingredients in commercial sunscreens. Findings revealed the lack of photoprotective capability of organic active ingredients extensively used in sunscreens.

Advisor: Prof. Kwabena Bediako

University of California, Berkeley, Berkeley, CA  
Undergraduate Research Assistant, Sep. 2017 – May 2018  
Advisor: Prof. Mike Crommie

- Fabricated graphene monolayers, via chemical vapor deposition, on 100–340 nm electron beam evaporated Cu-SiO₂ substrate.

Research Intern, Oct. 2016 - Jan. 2017 

Advisor: Prof. Sarp Kaya
